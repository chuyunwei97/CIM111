Exhibitions

The permanent exhibitions of the Museum can be divided into two types, one is the as-was exhibition, one is the themed exhibitions. The as-was exhibitions present the rooms in similar manners as the imperial time.

Current Exhibitions

Lingering Voice of the Sages: The Culture of Qin

  • Palace of Accumulated Purity (Zhongcui gong)
  • Dates: 2019-11-29 through ~

The qin, or seven-string zither, is one of China’s oldest musical instruments, and an important part of the country’s traditional culture of rites and music. In ancient times the single character "qin", which now refers to a number of instruments, specifically meant the guqin: from this we see its significance. We hope that through our exhibition the culture of the qin finds yet more avid listeners!

Upcoming Exhibitions

The Fortune and Longevity of Sumeru: An Encounter between the Tashi Lhunpo Monastery and the Palace Museum

  • Meridian Gate (Wu men)
  • Dates: 2019-12-20 through 2020-02-29

Tibetan Buddhism has a long history and diversity of sects, one of which is the dominant Gelug school (Tibetan dGe-Lugs-Pa, Chinese Gelu pai) founded by Tsongkhapa Lobzang Drakpa. Also known as the Yellow Sect, this branch of Buddhism took on an important role in seventeenth century China.

Previous Exhibitions

Miracle of Chinese Culture: History and Culture of Yunju Temple

  • Southeast Corner Gallery inside the Gate of Supreme Harmony
  • Dates: 2019-09-10 through 2019-10-19

The exhibition presents the history of sutra engraving at Yunju Temple (Yunju si, lit. “Cloud Residence Temple”) in the following four sections: “Origins of Sutra Engraving”, “Millennium of Prodigious Endeavors”, “Gold Sutras of the Stupa”, and “Protection of Sutras and Treasures”.
